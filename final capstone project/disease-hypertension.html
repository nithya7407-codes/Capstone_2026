Hypertension

Also called the "silent killer," hypertension is a condition where blood pressure in the arteries is persistently elevated, increasing the risk of heart disease and stroke.

🔍 Symptoms

  • Often no symptoms (silent killer)
  • Severe headaches, especially in the morning
  • Shortness of breath or chest pain
  • Nosebleeds in severe cases
  • Dizziness and blurred vision
  • Irregular heartbeat or pounding sensation

⚡ Causes

  • Primary: No known direct cause (genetic + lifestyle)
  • Secondary: Kidney disease, thyroid issues, medication
  • Excessive salt and sodium intake
  • Chronic stress and poor sleep habits
  • Obesity and lack of physical exercise

⚠️ Risk Factors

  • Age above 55 (men) or 65 (women)
  • Family history of hypertension
  • Being overweight or obese
  • Smoking and excessive alcohol use
  • High-sodium diet and sedentary lifestyle
  • Chronic stress or anxiety

🛡️ Prevention

  • Reduce daily salt intake to below 5g/day
  • Exercise 150 minutes per week (moderate intensity)
  • Maintain healthy weight and BMI
  • Limit alcohol to moderate amounts
  • Quit smoking completely
  • Manage stress with yoga, meditation, or breathing

💊 Management

  • Take prescribed antihypertensive medications regularly
  • Monitor blood pressure at home (target: below 120/80)
  • Follow a DASH diet (fruits, vegetables, low-fat dairy)
  • Reduce caffeine consumption
  • Regular follow-ups with your cardiologist or GP

🏥 When to See a Doctor

  • Blood pressure consistently above 140/90 mmHg
  • Sudden severe headache or chest pain
  • Sudden vision changes or difficulty speaking
  • Numbness or weakness in face/arm/leg
  • If on medication and not seeing improvement
⚠️ Important: Hypertension often has no symptoms. Regular blood pressure checks are the only way to detect it early. Consult a doctor for proper diagnosis.
