Water & Hydration

Water makes up about 60% of the human body and is involved in every single physiological process. Staying properly hydrated is one of the simplest and most powerful things you can do for your health.

✅ Why Hydration Matters

  • Regulates body temperature through sweating and respiration
  • Transports nutrients and oxygen to cells throughout the body
  • Flushes out waste and toxins via urine and sweat
  • Lubricates joints and cushions organs and tissues
  • Supports digestion and prevents constipation
  • Improves concentration, memory, and mood
  • Maintains healthy skin and prevents dryness

📊 Daily Water Intake

  • General adult (men): ~3.7 litres/day total fluid
  • General adult (women): ~2.7 litres/day total fluid
  • Hot climates like Chennai: add 0.5–1 litre extra
  • During exercise: drink 200–300 ml every 15–20 min
  • About 20% of water comes from food (fruits, vegetables)
  • Simple rule: drink when thirsty, aim for pale yellow urine

⚠️ Signs of Dehydration

  • Dark yellow or strong-smelling urine
  • Dry mouth, lips, and skin
  • Headache, dizziness, or lightheadedness
  • Fatigue and reduced concentration
  • Rapid heartbeat in severe cases
  • Muscle cramps during or after exercise

💡 Hydration Tips

  • Start your day with a glass of water before tea or coffee
  • Carry a reusable water bottle everywhere you go
  • Eat water-rich foods: cucumber, watermelon, oranges
  • Set hourly reminders to drink on busy or sedentary days
  • Limit sugary drinks and excess caffeine (dehydrating)
  • Increase intake during summer, illness, or intense activity
