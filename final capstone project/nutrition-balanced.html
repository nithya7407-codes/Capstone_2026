Balanced Diet

A balanced diet provides all the essential nutrients — carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, minerals, and water — in the right proportions to maintain health, energy, and prevent chronic disease.

🌾 The 5 Food Groups

  • Grains & Cereals: rice, wheat, oats, millets — energy source
  • Vegetables & Fruits: vitamins, minerals, fibre, antioxidants
  • Protein foods: dal, eggs, meat, fish, legumes, tofu
  • Dairy or alternatives: milk, curd, paneer, soy milk
  • Healthy fats: nuts, seeds, olive oil, avocado

✅ Benefits of Eating Balanced

  • Maintains healthy body weight and reduces obesity risk
  • Reduces risk of diabetes, heart disease, and hypertension
  • Boosts immunity and speeds recovery from illness
  • Supports mental health, mood, and cognitive function
  • Provides sustained energy throughout the day
  • Improves gut health and digestive regularity

🍱 Sample Indian Balanced Meal

  • Breakfast: Oats porridge + banana + a glass of milk
  • Mid-morning: Handful of nuts or a fruit
  • Lunch: Brown rice + dal + sabzi + curd + salad
  • Evening snack: Sprouts chaat or roasted chickpeas
  • Dinner: 2 rotis + paneer/fish curry + vegetable + lassi
  • Hydrate with 2–3 litres of water throughout the day

🚫 Foods to Limit

  • Ultra-processed foods: chips, biscuits, instant noodles
  • Sugary drinks: sodas, packaged juices, energy drinks
  • Excess refined carbs: white bread, maida products
  • Deep-fried foods consumed daily
  • Excess salt (more than 5g/day increases BP risk)
  • Alcohol and tobacco — both damage nutrition absorption

📋 Key Dietary Principles

  • Eat at regular intervals — don't skip meals, especially breakfast
  • Portion control matters as much as food quality
  • Cook at home more often to control ingredients and oil use
  • Include seasonal, locally available produce for best nutrition
  • Read food labels and be aware of hidden sugars and sodium
  • Consult a registered dietitian for personalised meal planning
💡 Remember: There is no single "perfect" diet. The best diet is one you can sustain long-term. Small, consistent healthy choices make the biggest difference over time.
