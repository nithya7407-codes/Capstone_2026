Cycling

Cycling is a low-impact cardiovascular exercise that strengthens the legs, improves heart health, and burns calories effectively — whether outdoors or on a stationary bike.

✅ Benefits

  • Excellent cardiovascular workout with low joint impact
  • Strengthens quadriceps, hamstrings, calves, and glutes
  • Burns 400–600 calories per hour at moderate pace
  • Improves lung capacity and oxygen efficiency
  • Reduces risk of heart disease, stroke, and diabetes
  • Boosts mood through endorphin release
  • Eco-friendly commute option with health benefits

⏱️ Recommended Duration

  • Beginners: 15–20 minutes, 3 times per week
  • Intermediate: 30–45 minutes, 4–5 times per week
  • Leisurely cycling: 12–16 km/h (light exercise)
  • Moderate pace: 19–24 km/h (aerobic zone)
  • Aim for 150 minutes of moderate cycling per week (WHO)

👥 Who Can Do It

  • Suitable for most ages including older adults
  • Good for people with knee pain (lower impact than running)
  • Those managing obesity or wanting gradual weight loss
  • Individuals with mild cardiovascular issues (with doctor approval)
  • People who find running too intense or high-impact

⚠️ Safety Tips

  • Always wear a properly fitted helmet while cycling outdoors
  • Adjust seat height so legs are nearly fully extended at bottom
  • Use lights and reflective gear when cycling in low visibility
  • Stay hydrated — drink water every 15–20 minutes
  • Follow traffic rules and use designated cycle lanes where available
  • Warm up with slow cycling for 5 minutes before increasing pace
