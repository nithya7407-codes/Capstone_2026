Strength Training

Strength (resistance) training uses weights, bands, or body weight to build muscle mass, improve bone density, and boost metabolism — delivering benefits that go far beyond appearance.

✅ Benefits

  • Increases muscle mass and metabolic rate (burns more at rest)
  • Strengthens bones and reduces risk of osteoporosis
  • Improves insulin sensitivity and helps manage blood sugar
  • Reduces body fat while preserving lean muscle tissue
  • Enhances posture, balance, and functional movement
  • Boosts confidence, mood, and mental resilience
  • Reduces risk of injury in daily activities and sports

⏱️ Recommended Duration

  • Beginners: 2 sessions per week, 30–40 minutes each
  • Intermediate: 3–4 sessions per week, 45–60 minutes each
  • Allow 48 hours of rest between working the same muscle group
  • Include compound movements: squats, deadlifts, push-ups, rows
  • Progressive overload: gradually increase weight or reps over time

👥 Who Can Do It

  • Adults of all ages (seniors benefit greatly from muscle preservation)
  • People managing obesity or metabolic syndrome
  • Those with type 2 diabetes (improves glucose control)
  • Women — strength training does not cause bulk, only tone
  • Anyone wanting to improve daily functional strength and mobility

⚠️ Safety Tips

  • Learn proper form before adding heavy weights — technique first
  • Always warm up with 5–10 minutes of light cardio before lifting
  • Use a spotter for heavy barbell exercises like bench press or squat
  • Do not hold your breath — exhale on the exertion phase
  • Rest and sleep are when muscles grow — recovery is not optional
  • If you feel sharp joint pain (not muscle burn), stop immediately
🏠
No Gym NeededPush-ups, squats, lunges, planks, and dips are effective bodyweight exercises you can do anywhere at home.
🥩
Fuel Your MusclesConsume adequate protein (1.2–1.6g/kg body weight) to support muscle repair and growth after training.
😴
Rest to GrowMuscles are built during rest, not in the gym. 7–9 hours of sleep accelerates recovery and performance gains.
