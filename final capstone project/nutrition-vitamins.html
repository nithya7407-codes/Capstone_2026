Vitamins

Vitamins are essential micronutrients that the body needs in small amounts to function properly. They support immunity, energy production, bone health, and hundreds of biochemical processes.

🌟 Key Vitamins & Their Role

  • Vitamin A – vision, skin health, immune defence
  • Vitamin B12 – nerve function, red blood cell formation
  • Vitamin C – immunity, collagen synthesis, antioxidant
  • Vitamin D – calcium absorption, bone strength, mood
  • Vitamin E – antioxidant, skin health, cell protection
  • Vitamin K – blood clotting and bone metabolism
  • Folate (B9) – cell division, critical during pregnancy

🍽️ Food Sources

  • Vitamin A: carrots, sweet potato, spinach, liver
  • Vitamin B12: eggs, dairy, meat, fish (plant sources need supplements)
  • Vitamin C: oranges, guava, amla, bell peppers, tomatoes
  • Vitamin D: sunlight exposure, fatty fish, fortified milk
  • Vitamin E: almonds, sunflower oil, peanuts, avocado
  • Folate: lentils, spinach, broccoli, fortified cereals

📊 Daily Requirements (Adults)

  • Vitamin A: 700–900 mcg RAE/day
  • Vitamin B12: 2.4 mcg/day (higher for elderly)
  • Vitamin C: 65–90 mg/day (smokers need more)
  • Vitamin D: 600–800 IU/day (higher in deficiency)
  • Vitamin E: 15 mg/day
  • Folate: 400 mcg/day (600 mcg during pregnancy)

⚠️ Deficiency Signs

  • Vitamin A deficiency: night blindness, dry skin
  • Vitamin B12 deficiency: anaemia, tingling, fatigue
  • Vitamin C deficiency: bleeding gums, slow wound healing
  • Vitamin D deficiency: weak bones, muscle pain, depression
  • Folate deficiency: neural tube defects, megaloblastic anaemia
  • Always consult a doctor before taking supplements
⚠️ Note: Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K) can accumulate to toxic levels if over-supplemented. Always get a blood test before starting supplements and follow medical advice.
