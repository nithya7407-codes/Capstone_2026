Thyroid Disorders

The thyroid gland regulates metabolism, energy, and hormones. Disorders like hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism affect millions and can impact weight, mood, and energy levels.

🔍 Symptoms

  • Unexplained weight gain or loss
  • Fatigue, weakness, or feeling cold
  • Hair thinning or loss
  • Irregular heartbeat (hyperthyroid)
  • Mood swings, depression, or anxiety
  • Swelling in the neck (goitre)

⚡ Causes

  • Autoimmune conditions (Hashimoto's, Graves' disease)
  • Iodine deficiency or excess
  • Radiation therapy to the neck area
  • Certain medications (lithium, amiodarone)
  • Pregnancy and hormonal changes

⚠️ Risk Factors

  • Female gender (women are 5–8× more likely)
  • Family history of thyroid disease
  • Age over 60
  • Personal history of autoimmune conditions
  • Iodine-deficient diet

🛡️ Prevention & Management

  • Consume iodine-rich foods (seafood, iodized salt)
  • Regular thyroid function tests (TSH, T3, T4)
  • Take prescribed medications consistently (Levothyroxine)
  • Manage stress and get adequate sleep
  • Avoid excessive soy and raw cruciferous vegetables

🏥 When to See a Doctor

  • Persistent fatigue or unexplained weight changes
  • Visible swelling in the neck region
  • Rapid or irregular heartbeat
  • Feeling constantly cold or overheated for no reason
  • Significant mood changes or depression
⚠️ Note: Thyroid disorders are highly treatable when diagnosed early. See an endocrinologist for proper testing and a personalised treatment plan.
