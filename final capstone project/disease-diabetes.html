Diabetes Mellitus

A chronic condition where the body cannot properly regulate blood sugar (glucose) due to insufficient insulin production or ineffective insulin use.

🔍 Symptoms

  • Frequent urination, especially at night
  • Excessive thirst and dry mouth
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Blurred vision and fatigue
  • Slow-healing wounds or infections
  • Tingling or numbness in hands/feet

⚡ Causes

  • Type 1: Autoimmune destruction of insulin-producing cells
  • Type 2: Insulin resistance due to lifestyle and genetics
  • Gestational: Hormonal changes during pregnancy
  • Obesity and physical inactivity
  • High-sugar, high-fat diet over long periods

⚠️ Risk Factors

  • Family history of diabetes
  • Being overweight or obese (BMI > 25)
  • Sedentary lifestyle with no exercise
  • Age above 45 for Type 2 diabetes
  • High blood pressure or cholesterol
  • History of gestational diabetes

🛡️ Prevention

  • Maintain a healthy body weight (BMI 18.5–24.9)
  • Exercise at least 30 minutes most days
  • Eat whole grains, vegetables, and lean proteins
  • Avoid sugary drinks and processed foods
  • Get regular blood sugar screenings annually
  • Quit smoking and limit alcohol intake

💊 Management

  • Monitor blood glucose levels regularly at home
  • Take prescribed medications (Metformin, insulin) as directed
  • Follow a diabetic-friendly low-GI diet
  • Stay physically active to improve insulin sensitivity
  • Regular check-ups with doctor every 3–6 months
  • Manage stress, as it raises blood sugar levels

🏥 When to See a Doctor

  • Blood sugar levels consistently above 126 mg/dL fasting
  • Symptoms of hypoglycemia (shaking, confusion, sweating)
  • Sudden vision changes or loss
  • Non-healing foot wounds or infections
  • If pregnant and at risk of gestational diabetes
  • Any signs of kidney or nerve complications
⚠️ Important: This page is for educational awareness only. Please consult a certified doctor or endocrinologist for diagnosis and treatment plans.
