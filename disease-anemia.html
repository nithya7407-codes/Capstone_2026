Anaemia

Anaemia occurs when the body doesn't have enough healthy red blood cells to carry adequate oxygen to body tissues, causing fatigue, weakness, and reduced physical performance.

🔍 Symptoms

  • Persistent fatigue and weakness
  • Pale or yellowish skin
  • Shortness of breath on mild exertion
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Cold hands and feet
  • Brittle nails and hair loss
  • Irregular or fast heartbeat

⚡ Causes

  • Iron deficiency (most common cause worldwide)
  • Vitamin B12 or folate deficiency
  • Chronic diseases (kidney disease, cancer)
  • Inherited conditions (sickle cell, thalassaemia)
  • Heavy menstrual bleeding in women
  • Poor dietary intake of iron-rich foods

⚠️ Risk Factors

  • Women of childbearing age (due to menstruation)
  • Pregnancy (increased iron demand)
  • Vegetarian or vegan diet without supplementation
  • Children in rapid growth phases
  • Elderly individuals with poor dietary intake

🛡️ Prevention

  • Eat iron-rich foods: spinach, lentils, red meat, beans
  • Consume Vitamin C with meals to enhance iron absorption
  • Avoid tea and coffee with meals (reduce iron absorption)
  • Take prescribed iron supplements if deficient
  • Regular blood tests (CBC) to monitor haemoglobin levels

💊 Management

  • Iron supplements (ferrous sulphate) as directed
  • Vitamin B12 injections or supplements if deficient
  • Address underlying cause (treat bleeding, chronic disease)
  • Dietary modifications to include more iron sources
  • Blood transfusion in severe cases (hospital setting)

🏥 When to See a Doctor

  • Persistent fatigue not explained by lifestyle
  • Haemoglobin below 12 g/dL (women) or 13.5 g/dL (men)
  • Pica (craving for non-food substances like ice or clay)
  • Chest pain or severe shortness of breath
  • Fainting spells or extreme dizziness
⚠️ Note: Anaemia is very common in India, especially among women. A simple blood test can confirm it. Consult a doctor before starting any iron supplementation.
