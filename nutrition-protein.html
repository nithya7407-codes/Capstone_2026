Proteins

Proteins are essential macronutrients made of amino acids. They are the building blocks of muscles, enzymes, hormones, and immune cells — critical for growth, repair, and everyday body functions.

✅ Why Proteins Are Important

  • Build and repair muscles, tissues, and organs
  • Produce enzymes and hormones (insulin, growth hormone)
  • Support immune system function and antibody production
  • Transport oxygen in blood (haemoglobin is a protein)
  • Provide satiety, helping with weight management
  • Maintain fluid balance and pH levels in the body

🍽️ Food Sources

  • Animal: eggs, chicken, fish, lean meat, dairy (milk, paneer)
  • Plant-based: lentils (dal), chickpeas, kidney beans, tofu
  • Nuts and seeds: almonds, peanuts, chia, sunflower seeds
  • Soy products: soya chunks, edamame, soy milk
  • Whole grains: quinoa, amaranth (contain some protein)

📊 Daily Requirements

  • General adult: 0.8g per kg of body weight/day
  • Active individuals: 1.2–1.6g per kg body weight/day
  • Athletes / bodybuilders: up to 2g per kg body weight/day
  • Example: 60 kg person needs ~48–96g protein/day
  • Spread across meals for best absorption

⚠️ Deficiency & Excess

  • Deficiency: muscle loss, weakness, poor immunity, slow healing
  • Kwashiorkor: severe protein deficiency (swelling, stunted growth)
  • Excess protein: strain on kidneys in those with kidney issues
  • Balance is key — consult a dietitian for personalised needs
  • Choose lean, whole-food protein sources where possible
