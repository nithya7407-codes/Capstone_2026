Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety disorders are the most common mental health conditions, involving excessive fear, worry, or nervousness that interferes with daily activities and quality of life.

🔍 Symptoms

  • Persistent and excessive worry about everyday situations
  • Rapid heartbeat, sweating, and trembling
  • Shortness of breath or chest tightness
  • Restlessness and difficulty concentrating
  • Sleep problems and muscle tension
  • Avoidance of triggers (social situations, places)

⚡ Causes

  • Genetic predisposition to anxiety and stress
  • Traumatic or stressful life experiences
  • Chronic medical conditions (heart disease, thyroid)
  • Excessive caffeine or substance use
  • Certain medications or withdrawal effects

⚠️ Risk Factors

  • Family history of anxiety or mental health issues
  • Childhood trauma or adverse experiences
  • High-stress occupations or environments
  • Introversion combined with low social support
  • Chronic physical health conditions

🛡️ Coping Strategies

  • Practice deep breathing (4-7-8 technique)
  • Regular aerobic exercise reduces anxiety significantly
  • Limit caffeine and sugar in your daily diet
  • Maintain a structured daily routine and sleep schedule
  • Grounding techniques (5-4-3-2-1 sensory method)
  • Mindfulness and progressive muscle relaxation

💊 Treatment

  • Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) — most effective
  • Medication (SSRIs, benzodiazepines) as prescribed
  • Regular therapy sessions with a psychologist
  • Support groups and peer sharing
  • Lifestyle changes: sleep hygiene and stress reduction

🏥 When to Seek Help

  • Anxiety interfering with work or relationships
  • Panic attacks that occur without warning
  • Using substances to cope with anxiety
  • Feeling unable to leave home or social settings
  • Physical symptoms with no medical explanation
💙 Note: Anxiety is treatable. Early intervention leads to much better outcomes. Speak to a licensed mental health professional — you don't have to manage this alone.
