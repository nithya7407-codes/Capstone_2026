Depression

Depression is a serious mental health condition characterised by persistent sadness, loss of interest, and a range of emotional and physical problems that significantly impact daily life.

🔍 Symptoms

  • Persistent sadness or empty feeling most days
  • Loss of interest in hobbies and activities
  • Changes in appetite and unexplained weight changes
  • Sleep disturbances (insomnia or oversleeping)
  • Fatigue and lack of energy for daily tasks
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Feelings of worthlessness or guilt

⚡ Causes

  • Imbalance in brain chemicals (serotonin, dopamine)
  • Traumatic life events or chronic stress
  • Family history of depression
  • Chronic illness or physical health problems
  • Substance abuse or alcohol dependence
  • Social isolation and lack of support

⚠️ Risk Factors

  • History of depression or other mental health issues
  • Major life changes or trauma
  • Low self-esteem or negative thinking patterns
  • Certain medications (hormones, steroids)
  • Nutritional deficiencies (Vitamin D, B12)

🛡️ Prevention & Lifestyle

  • Maintain regular physical activity and exercise
  • Build strong social connections and support networks
  • Practice mindfulness, meditation, or journaling
  • Get 7–9 hours of quality sleep each night
  • Limit alcohol and avoid recreational drugs
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in omega-3 and whole foods

💊 Management

  • Psychotherapy (Cognitive Behavioural Therapy is effective)
  • Antidepressant medications as prescribed by a doctor
  • Regular check-ins with a mental health professional
  • Stay connected with loved ones — do not isolate
  • Set small daily goals to maintain structure and routine

🏥 When to Seek Help

  • Symptoms lasting more than two weeks
  • Inability to function at work or in relationships
  • Thoughts of self-harm or hopelessness
  • Substance use as a coping mechanism
  • Any mental health crisis — seek immediate support
