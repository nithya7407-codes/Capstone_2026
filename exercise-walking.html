Walking

One of the most accessible forms of exercise. A regular walking routine can dramatically improve cardiovascular health, mood, weight management, and overall well-being — for people of all ages.

✅ Benefits

  • Improves heart and lung health (cardiovascular fitness)
  • Helps with weight management and calorie burning
  • Reduces risk of Type 2 diabetes and hypertension
  • Strengthens bones and joints, reduces arthritis pain
  • Boosts mood, reduces depression and anxiety
  • Improves sleep quality and energy levels

⏱️ Recommended Duration

  • Beginners: 15–20 minutes daily at comfortable pace
  • Intermediate: 30 minutes brisk walking most days
  • Goal: 10,000 steps per day (approx. 7–8 km)
  • WHO recommends 150 min moderate activity per week
  • Split into 3 × 10-minute walks if needed

👥 Who Can Do It

  • Suitable for all ages — children to elderly
  • Safe for people with mild arthritis or joint pain
  • Ideal for those new to exercise or recovering from illness
  • Pregnant women (with doctor's advice)
  • Those with controlled diabetes or hypertension

⚠️ Safety Tips

  • Wear supportive, well-fitting footwear
  • Walk on flat, safe surfaces especially when starting
  • Stay hydrated — carry water for longer walks
  • Warm up with 5 minutes of slow walking first
  • Avoid walking during extreme heat — morning is best
  • Consult a doctor before intense walking if you have health conditions
